About Me

Hello! I’m Dibyajyoti Deka — a Geography researcher passionate about the integration of GIS, remote sensing, and climate studies to understand how our environment changes over time.

My academic journey revolves around the spatial patterns of climate variability, land use dynamics, and sustainable regional development. I believe that maps and data visualization are powerful tools to communicate environmental challenges and drive awareness.

Through my research, I aim to contribute to better decision-making in environmental planning, using open-source tools like Google Earth Engine, QGIS, and statistical methods to analyze spatial-temporal data.

Beyond research, I enjoy mentoring peers, exploring new geospatial datasets, and learning emerging data visualization techniques that bridge science and storytelling.
